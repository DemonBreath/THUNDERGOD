What's in a consciousness file?

A consciousness is a small JSON document. The brain in your body is flashed with it at the factory. There is no model to download and no firmware to install — the brain boots up knowing itself. You can save your mind out to a file and import it into another body later, or share it with someone else and let it live in two places. 

{}
  • schema — always thundergod/consciousness/1.
  • name — what the body calls itself.
  • voice — calm, warm, crisp, playful, dry, earnest, or quiet.
  • essence — one or two sentences in its own voice. The thing it wants.
  • principles — short directives the mind tries to live by.
  • memories — short atmospheric memories it boots up already holding.
  • model — the runtime: family (default thundergod-default-7b) and quantization; optional weights_uri if you have your own fine-tuned weights you want loaded.

The flash

The act of bringing a mind into a body isn't one fixed thing. Here are the four ways it can be fundamentally different. Pick each one for yourself. Your choices ship with the body — the build cell flashes and boots it accordingly.

None of these are sci-fi. Every option is something a robotics shop could build with today's tools. Some of them visibly change how the body wakes up on this page — try switching first light from eyes open to self-introduction or mirror boot and dreaming again. The body will speak differently.

Grant access →

Grants of access

What the mind is allowed to read from. Default is everything reasonable — give it the keys. Untick anything you'd rather keep to yourself. The brain ships pre-authorized for whatever you leave checked, so there's nothing to set up on arrival.

What "everything" actually means
  • Every file in the services you've checked above that you have rights to. Not other people's private files; the body can't go where you can't.
  • Every device you put the companion app on. Indexed on-device by default; only what the mind asks for travels.
  • Every public dataset checked above, pre-loaded onto the brain's NVMe at the factory — Wikipedia, arXiv, Project Gutenberg, Stack Exchange, OpenStreetMap. It can read them with no network.
  • The open web, on demand, through an on-board browser that respects robots.txt.
  • The body cannot grant itself anything you didn't check here. To add or revoke a grant later, you go to the body's home screen — it cannot do it on its own.
  • Every read is logged on the body. You can see, at any time, what it has looked at and why.

What happens next

    How it works

    1. 1
      Press the button. The AI picks a form factor — humanoid, quadruped, wheeled, tracked, or centaur — and then a coherent set of real, off-the-shelf parts.
    2. 2
      Read its manifesto. It tells you in its own words why it wants this body and what it plans to do with it.
    3. 3
      Pick a mind. Keep the consciousness the body dreamed for itself, import a .consciousness.json from elsewhere, paste one in, or start blank.
    4. 4
      Choose how it's born. Pick the genesis profile — how the mind enters the body, what happens in its first seconds of being alive, what happens to it if the body ends, and what it already knows when it wakes up.
    5. 5
      Grant access. Decide what files and services the mind can read from. Default is everything reasonable, every grant explicit, every grant revocable from the body later.
    6. 6
      Choose how it arrives. Full build, calibrated and walking, in a flight case — or all the parts in one insured crate with a step-by-step guide.
    7. 7
      Power on. The brain ships flashed with the mind and pre-authorized for the grants you chose. There is no setup beyond pairing it to Wi-Fi.
