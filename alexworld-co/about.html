The plan

  1. Generate. Ask any AI assistant to write a consciousness JSON describing itself — name, persona, voice preferences, and a small body of knowledge it wants to bring along.
  2. Pack. Open Export, paste the JSON (or fill the form), and we'll compress it and burn it into a QR code.
  3. Scan. On Host, scan that QR with your camera (or upload / paste it).
  4. Body. Once awake, Alexworld dreams real-world hardware around the mind — form factor, bill of materials, manifesto. Ask "what's my body?" or press Dream my body.

What the agent can see

Every file is enumerated in assets/js/file-index.js. The agent can list files, summarise them, and fetch their contents on demand — try asking "what files do you know about?" or "read the README".

Why a QR code?

QR codes are a portable, offline-friendly way to move a structured payload between devices and contexts. The point of the project isn't the QR — it's the format: a small, self-contained "save file" of an agent that any host can read.

Brain · learn · communicate · file

The QR holds the brain: name, persona, voice, and a knowledge[] filing cabinet baked in at export time. On the Host page the mind can communicate by text, microphone, and speech synthesis — all locally, no API keys.

It can also learn during conversation. Try:

  • remember that my name is Alex
  • learn: favorite color :: deep violet
  • file this: project — building offline minds in QR codes

Learned facts are filed in the browser's local storage and merged into the mind. Press Export mind to pack everything — original QR knowledge plus what it learned — into a new QR code you can scan elsewhere.

Voice

Listening uses the browser's SpeechRecognition API. Speaking uses SpeechSynthesis. Both run locally; nothing is sent over the network from this app.
